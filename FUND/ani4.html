Hi, I’m Janeille and I’m starting a fundraiser for my dog Harold, a 12 year old rescued Shih Tzu mix. Harold has been through so much in the five years since I adopted him, and the recent discovery of a mass in his small intestine and the need for surgery has been a huge unexpected expense. I adopted Harold on August 11, 2019, as a morbidly obese (25 pounds) euthanasia list survivor. Over the course of a year, he lost over 10 pounds and became a happy, healthier dog. Today he weighs just over 13 pounds and is at his ideal weight. After Harold lost the excess weight, there were other health issues that came up, possibly as a result of his prior obesity. Harold has had gastrointestinal issues and a ruptured cranial cruciate ligament (CCL) in his knee. His torn ligament required surgery, physical therapy and acupuncture to heal. Harold’s health and comfort are top priorities for me, and I hope that I can continue to give him (and Alfred, our hospice foster dog) a healthy, happy life. Harold’s gastrointestinal issues flared up again in recent weeks, and after $3,500 of testing and diagnostics, a tumor was discovered in his small intestine on Tuesday, 8/6/24. This tumor requires removal in a timely manner, so Harold is having surgery today. The estimate for his surgery is $9,279.47 - $11,135.36. I know, it’s a huge number. I’ve had some dear friends ask if there’s anything they can do to help, and so very humbly, this is my plea for help to fund Harold’s surgery. Harold and I are so appreciative of your generosity and positive thoughts while we navigate the next chapter of his health care journey. I will post updates here as well as IG (hefty_harold). Thank you, friends and family!
